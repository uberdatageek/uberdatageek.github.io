Global Fight Against Malaria in Africa

Malaria is both preventable and curable

What is Malaria?

Malaria is caused by the Plasmodium parasite and is transmitted by female Anopheles mosquitoes which bite between dusk and dawn.
Symptoms: People infected with malaria often experience fever, chills and flu-like illness at first.
Who's at risk: Young children, pregnant women and non-immune travellers from malaria-free areas are highly vulnerable.
The Numbers: Roughly 3.2 billion people are at risk of Malaria, with 214 million cases reported in 2015.

Malaria between 2000 & 2015

Globally reported cases of malaria declined between 2000 & 2015
37% decline in malaria cases
60% decline in global malaria mortality rates
